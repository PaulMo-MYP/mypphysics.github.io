Each dot in the chamber is one radioactive nucleus. Every animation frame, every undecayed dot has a small, independent chance of decaying, governed by the decay constant λ = ln(2) / T½. This is why the measured (solid) line wobbles around the smooth theoretical (dashed) curve — real decay is random, not exact.
